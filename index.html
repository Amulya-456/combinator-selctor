States of Matter

Matter can transition between different states, also known as phases, depending on the addition or removal of energy (typically heat):

Solids

Particles are packed tightly together. They maintain a fixed shape and volume.

Liquids

Particles are close together but can move past one another. They have a fixed volume but take the shape of their container.

Gases

Particles are far apart and move freely. They have neither a fixed shape nor a fixed volume.
